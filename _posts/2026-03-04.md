Hello
My name is A and I am a hospitalist in the United States. The website was built using AI but all blog posts, ideas and implementation of data analysis was done by me, a human being. The audience of this website is intended to be physicians.
Since the launch of the o3 reasoning model by OpenAI in april of 2025, I have been blown away by the diagnostic and literature search capabilities of artificial intelligence- specifically the large language models. However, they primarily arrive at diagnosis using diagnostic criteria and reasoning using physiology much like human physicians. During residency and after, I found certain edge cases where human physicians performed better than the models- and I suspect this is due to the limitations of the transformer architecture in truly estimating probablities of a disease before and after tests. 
Using visual methods, I plan to show you how a new method (really an old method that has been forgotten in the age of transformer based language models) can overcome these problems and capture more accurate diagnoses.

Lets jump into our first scenario- a patient presenting with shortness of breath to the emergency room in the famous database MIMIC IV.
